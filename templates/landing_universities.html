Students often know the theory, but struggle with structure

One of the hardest parts of physiotherapy education is helping students move from isolated facts to a repeatable reasoning process. They may know the concepts, but still miss key links between history, flags, diagnosis, goals, and treatment planning. PhysiologicPRISM gives them a framework to think through the case in order.

A teaching tool for real clinical reasoning

PhysiologicPRISM breaks the assessment journey into guided modules so students can learn how each decision connects to the next. This supports clearer thinking, more consistent case work, and a stronger bridge between theory and practice.

Structured subjective assessment

Support history-taking and ICF-informed reasoning with a more organized process.

Flags and diagnostic thinking

Help students identify relevant warning signs and connect findings to clinical interpretation.

Goals, treatment, and follow-up

Teach students how to convert assessment findings into SMART goals, intervention plans, and ongoing review.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this meant to replace teaching?

No. It is designed to support teaching by giving students a structured framework for applying what faculty already teach.

Which parts of training does it support?

It is especially relevant for subjective assessment, clinical reasoning, flags, diagnosis, goal setting, treatment planning, and follow-up thinking.

Can it be used for case-based learning?

Yes. The modular workflow makes it well suited for guided case work and structured discussion.

Why would a university adopt this early?

Because it helps standardize reasoning practice and gives students a more usable bridge between theory and real-world assessment.
