PhysiologicPRISM Pilot Program

Evaluate structured, AI-guided clinical reasoning in your real-world setting.

The PhysiologicPRISM pilot program is designed for clinics, universities, and educational platforms that want to explore how a structured clinical reasoning workflow and AI guidance can improve documentation quality, consistency, and learning outcomes — before committing to a full rollout.

Who is the pilot for?

  • Physiotherapy clinics and hospitals standardising assessment quality
  • Universities and educators teaching ICF, clinical reasoning, and evidence-based practice
  • Platforms like Physiopedia exploring practical tools to complement educational content
  • Research teams studying clinical reasoning, documentation, or outcomes

Pilot structure at a glance

Duration:
30 days
Participants:
~30 users
  • 10 academic / research users
  • 15 clinical practitioners
  • 5 new graduates / final-year students
Scope:
Full access to PhysiologicPRISM's assessment workflow, AI guidance, and reporting
Support:
Onboarding resources, email support, and optional weekly check-ins

What your team gets during the pilot

  • Full access to the PhysiologicPRISM assessment workflow
  • Up to 200 AI-assisted interactions per user per month
  • Structured documentation from history to follow-up
  • Priority support for setup and questions
  • Option to co-design features or integrations based on feedback

What we'll measure together

The goal of the pilot is to understand how PhysiologicPRISM affects clinical reasoning, documentation, and user experience — not to force a specific outcome.

  • Completeness of assessments
  • Confidence using ICF and clinical reasoning frameworks
  • Perceived usefulness of AI suggestions
  • Perceived improvement in legal/compliance confidence
  • Willingness to keep using PhysiologicPRISM after the pilot

Aggregated, anonymised insights can be shared back with you as a report, and used as a foundation for joint case studies or publications if desired.

How the pilot works

  1. Intro call
    We understand your context and goals (clinic, university, or platform).
  2. Pilot design
    We confirm the number and type of users, start date, and success metrics.
  3. Onboarding
    Users receive access, a short demo video, and quick-start guidance.
  4. Active use (30 days)
    Participants use PhysiologicPRISM with real cases in their setting.
  5. Feedback & review
    We gather feedback, usage data, and discuss outcomes.
  6. Next steps
    If the pilot is successful, we explore a long-term partnership or rollout.
